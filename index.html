Hi, I'm Isaac Murchie. I am a PhD candidate in Sanskrit poetry and poetics in the Department of South and Southeast Asian Studies at the University of California, Berkeley. My dissertation, entitled "Crimes Against Aesthetics: Pattern Poetry (Citra-kāvya) in Sanskrit literary culture," looks at structural and algorithmic figures of speech as an evolving phenomenon in the Sanskrit literary world, and asks how these figures, which fundamentally contradict the dominant aesthetic theories of the day, affect what poetry is and how it is approached.

I am also a software developer, interested in how computers can be harnessed to allow people to manage and disseminate their interests and the bodies of knowledge those interests generate.
